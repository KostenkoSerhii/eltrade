  • Bitcoin is the first digital currency that managed to avoid banks and payment processors as only an Internet connection is needed to get involved. Bitcoin has become a decentralized, international market. And here there are the instructions for your start with Bitcoin:

    You can buy small amounts of Bitcoin online. If you are buying under $2000 of Bitcoin or are new to crypto currencies try one of the combined wallet / bitcoin traders to get rolling. Examples include Coinbase and Xapo. You can buy a small amount of Bitcoin in exchange for an approximate 1% service charge. Like a bank, they will also store your bitcoin for you on their servers.

    You can buy large amounts of Bitcoin via a trading exchange. If you are buying over $2000 of Bitcoin, surely you are eager to take advantage of the lower commission rates offered on an exchange. These exchanges operate like a stock market with bid / ask spreads changing 24 hours a day. Making an account at an exchange is a similar process to opening a new bank account, you will have to tell your real personal data and contacts and send the money. Different countries and currencies have different Bitcoin exchanges that are best to use in each area:

    US Dollars to Bitcoin - Bitfinex, GDAX (owned by Coinbase)

    Euro to Bitcoin - Kraken

    Chinese Yuan to Bitcoin - BTCC, Huobi or OKCoin

    Today, there are many cities with Bitcoin ATMs where you can take money with a 5-8% fee. The list of ATMs you can find at CoinATMradar.com.

    LocalBitcoins presents offline bitcoin dealers near you who can sell you Bitcoin. Worldwide there are people who can load bitcoins onto your phone for a solid sum of money. But you’d better start with trading for small amounts of money in order to make sure your partner is well-trusted.

    You can set up your first Bitcoin wallet. If you are not tech-savvy you can keep your bitcoin in the account where you bought them. Practically the majority of bitcoin holders do just that. But the bitcoin attraction is in irrelevance of a that no third party to control for you. By the way, as exchanges store hundreds of millions of dollars of bitcoin, they are a coveted prize for hackers. Any it is wise to store your bitcoins on your own rather than trusting them to an exchange. Here is a list of wallets that will not give any third-party access to your bitcoins:

    Mobile Bitcoin Wallets are considered to be practical for storing small amounts of Bitcoin. Such wallets are less liable for malware than computers and create secure environment. The list of the better ones is below:

    Airbitz

    Mycelium

    BitPay

    Jaxx

    The ease of web wallets is in their online availability. You just need to set it up and log in. The most popular web wallet is: Blockchain.info

    If you are new to Bitcoin and aimed at a large of bitcoin then your choice is hardware wallets. It is considered to be the most troubleproof variant. The most popular wallet of such a type is Trezor Ledger

    Advanced wallet type is the originally intended for Bitcoin. Such a type won’t be suitable for newcomers. You'll have to run a local copy of the blockchain to keep your transactions anonymous. The software wallet includes:

    Armory Multibit
